The Challenge

To control advanced prostate cancer, suppressing serum testosterone is critical. Here is the 48-week clinical profile of 148 patients initiating therapy.

Twice-Yearly Dosing

Patients received injections at 24-week intervals (Week 0, 24, and 48). Orange bars indicate measurement weeks; Maroon bars represent the intervals between.

Expected Initial Surge

As is characteristic of GnRH agonists, a transient increase in testosterone—the "flare"—occurs immediately after the first dose (Week 2).

Rapid, Sustained Control

By Week 4, the mean testosterone level drops precipitously to 15.9 ng/dL, well below the standard castrate threshold (≤50 ng/dL).

Durable Efficacy

Efficacy does not wane over time. At the end of the 48-week study, the mean serum testosterone level was suppressed even further to 9.9 ng/dL.

Stability of Response

Rescaling the view to the 0–50 ng/dL range reveals the remarkable stability of the maintenance phase.

Achieving Deep Suppression

Modern urology looks beyond the 50 ng/dL standard. 94.1% of patients achieved Deep Suppression (≤20 ng/dL).
